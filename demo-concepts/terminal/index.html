about

I'm a CS student at NTNU. I was admitted to Carnegie Mellon's Information Systems program on Early Action — then stepped back from it. Walking away from a school I'd worked years to reach was the hardest thing I've done, and it taught me the lesson that runs through everything since: ability means little without discipline and ownership.

So I rebuilt, on my own terms. I went deep on the things I actually love — shipping products, and lately doing it with AI, which multiplied what a single person can make. I founded a community, created for an audience, and started turning ideas into working software the same week I have them.

The way I work now: see widely, lead with value, and help before I'm asked — opportunities tend to follow. I'd rather build something real and useful than talk about it.
